The Heart Behind the Words

As a Bachelor of Secondary Education Major in English, my journey has always been defined by a deep-seated reverence for communication. Since my first year of high school, poetry has been my sanctuary—a way to distill complex emotions into ink and paper.


I balance the structural rigor of a Licensed Professional Teacher with the boundless creativity of a lifelong writer. I don't just teach the language; I live it.

The Poetry Portfolio

Click a card to unveil the verses.

01. The Ink's First Breath

Fresh morning dew upon the page, A silent heart escapes its cage. The first line drawn in trembling light, To banish shadows of the night.

02. Syntax of the Soul

Verb and noun in sweet embrace, Finding rhythm, seeking grace. Structure born from chaotic thought, The very lessons that life has taught.

03. Midnight Reverie

When the world descends to hush, And colors fade to velvet plush. The moon becomes my only guide, With nowhere left for truth to hide.

04. The Educator's Creed

To plant a seed in fertile minds, To break the chains that ignorance binds. A torch held high in darkened halls, Responding when the future calls.

05. Fleeting Seasons

Autumn leaves and winter's frost, Counting all that we have lost. Yet spring arrives with emerald fire, Refueling every lost desire.

06. Metaphoric Skies

Clouds are merely heavy dreams, Tearing at the silver seams. The rain is but a sky's lament, For hours that were poorly spent.

07. The Gilded Quill

Summa gold and parchment white, Winning every silent fight. An odyssey of grit and grace, Running at a steady pace.

08. Echoes of High School

The girl I was is still within, Where all the stanzas first begin. A decade passed, the ink still flows, As every secret flower grows.

09. Urban Silence

Steel and stone and humming wire, Chasing every cold desire. But in the cracks, the wild weeds grow, Reminding us of what we know.

10. The Final Stanza

The book may close, the light may dim, Filled with hope up to the brim. For every end is just a start, Etched upon a grateful heart.
