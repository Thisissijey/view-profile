The Heart Behind the Pen

I am a 24-year-old Bachelor of Secondary Education Major in English graduate. My journey with language isn't just academic—it’s deeply personal. Since my first year of high school, poetry has been my primary lens for viewing the world.


I believe in the power of the written word to bridge the gap between human experience and silent emotion. While others find solace in the outdoors, I find my sanctuary in the rhythm of a well-placed stanza and the structured chaos of a blank page.

The Decadent Collection

10 Poems. 10 Worlds.

01. Ink & Ether

The nib bleeds black upon the stark white field, A surrender to the thoughts I cannot hold. Between the margins, secret truths are yielded, In characters of silver and of gold. No spoken breath could carry half the weight, Of what the silent parchment can translate.

02. Binary Heartbeat

One and zero, pulse and pause, Logic trapped in glass-walled laws. But even in the cold machine, Are ghosts of things that might have been. A flicker in the glowing blue, A digital search for something true.

03. The Educator’s Vow

To speak of syntax is to speak of life, To carve a path through ignorance and strife. I plant the seeds of grammar in the mind, So voices lost may finally be defined. The classroom is a garden, vast and deep, Where promises of future giants sleep.

04. Midnight in Manila

The city hums a frantic, neon hymn, While shadows dance upon the river’s rim. Traffic lights are rubies in the rain, Pulsing with a rhythmic, urban pain. We are but sparks within the concrete hive, Just trying, desperately, to feel alive.

05. Vintage Coffee

Bitter bean and steam that curls like smoke, A conversation shared before we broke. The porcelain is chipped around the edge, Like every hollow, half-forgotten pledge. Drink the dregs and let the morning come, Until the aching in the chest is numb.

06. The Unsent Letter

I folded up the sun and put it in an envelope, Tucked beside a fraying strand of desperate hope. I never licked the seal or wrote your name, Lest the paper burst into a sudden flame. It sits within the drawer of things unsaid, A quiet ghost I haven't quite put to bed.

07. Syntax of a Soul

Am I a noun? A static, solid thing? Or just a verb, a frantic, fleeting wing? Adjectives fail to capture who I feel, The adjectives are masks; the verbs are real. I am the action, not the hollow state, A living sentence moving toward its fate.

08. Loom of Time

Warp and weft, the seconds weave the cloth, Drawn toward the flame like any foolish moth. The pattern emerges in the gray and white, Stitched by the needles of the day and night. Do not fear the fraying at the end, For every broken thread has time to mend.

09. Architectural Silence

Columns of marble and arches of bone, Building a temple for being alone. No windows to watch as the seasons go by, No skylights to offer a piece of the sky. Only the echoes of steps on the floor, And the heavy, mahogany weight of the door.

10. The Final Stanza

The curtain falls, the ink begins to dry, A thousand poems beneath a paper sky. But though the book is closed and put away, The echoes of the verses choose to stay. For every ending is a door flung wide, To all the music waiting deep inside.
