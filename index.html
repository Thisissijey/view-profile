Academic Excellence

A Journey of Letters & Learning

I am a 24-year-old educator with a **Bachelor of Secondary Education Major in English**, graduating as **Summa Cum Laude**. My relationship with the written word began in my first year of high school, where I discovered that poetry isn't just a hobby—it's a way of breathing.


While many seek adventure in the mountains, I find my peaks in the rhythm of a well-placed stanza and my depths in the nuances of English literature.

The Poetry Portfolio

Ten distinct voices from a decade of writing.

I. The Chalk’s Echo

White dust on tired fingers, A symphony of scratching on the slate. To teach is to be a sculptor, Of a future we cannot yet dictate.

II. Midnight Ink

The moon is a comma in a sky of prose, Where the restless heart finally knows, That silence is a language rarely spoken, By those whose dreams remain unbroken.

III. Syntax of Soul

Subject, verb, and the object of my affection, Lost in the grammar of a quiet connection. No punctuation can halt this flow, Where logic ends and feelings grow.

IV. Graduation Gown

Heavy silk and gold-trimmed pride, Years of struggle tucked inside. The 'Summa' title is a heavy crown, Worn while the sun of youth goes down.

V. First Year High

A notebook hidden in a crowded bag, Before the spirit learned to sag. The very first rhyme, a clumsy beat, Made the world feel finally complete.

VI. Urban Rustle

Concrete veins and electric blood, The city drowning in a human flood. I find my forest in the library aisles, And travel across a thousand miles.

VII. Digital Dust

Cursors blink like a dying star, In a world where we forgot who we are. Pixels replace the scent of old paper, As memories turn to a flickering vapor.

VIII. The Editor's Red

Strike through the ego, bleed on the page, Wisdom is found in the pruning of age. Less is more when the truth is bare, A singular word is a silent prayer.

IX. Seasons of Blue

Not the ocean, but the shade of a bruise, The colors that we often choose, When the morning is cold and the coffee is black, And there is no road leading us back.

X. The Final Period

The pen rests now, its duty done, Under the light of a setting sun. The portfolio ends, but the story is wide, With millions of words still trapped inside.
